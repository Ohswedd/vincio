# Vincio examples

All examples run **fully offline** by default using the deterministic mock
provider — no API keys needed. To run against a real model:

```bash
export VINCIO_PROVIDER=openai VINCIO_MODEL=gpt-5.2-mini OPENAI_API_KEY=sk-...
```

| `74_cross_org_attestation_revocation_freshness.py` | cross-org attestation revocation & freshness — keeping a portable prior **current**: an attestation carries an issuer-declared validity window (`horizon_days`) bound into its signed hash, so against an as-of clock a **stale** attestation is excluded and pinpointed while an older one **decays** out of the pooled prior by an importer `half_life_days` (its evidence mass halved each half-life); an issuer signs a content-bound **`AttestationRevocation`** (`app.revoke_attestation` / `book.revoke()`) that withdraws or supersedes a prior attestation **by its hash**, offline-verifiable the way the attestation is, so the withdrawn claim is **excluded and pinpointed** (`AttestationVerdict.revoked` / `.stale`), never silently honored — and a forged revocation, or one naming another org's attestation, **cannot cancel a claim**; freshness and revocation fold into the existing **`combine_attestations(..., revocations=, as_of=)`** / `app.import_reputation` under the same **`[floor, 1]`** rule, never a hosted revocation service |
| `75_cross_org_reputation_gossip.py` | cross-org reputation gossip & attestation exchange — **discovering** who has attested a counterparty: an org exposes its earned standing as a queryable peer over A2A (**`app.serve_attestations`**) that, **pull never push**, answers a query with its *own* signed attestations and revocations; an importer with no local history pulls a **bounded** set of governed peers (**`app.gather_reputation`** / **`AttestationExchange`**), verifies every fetched artifact from the bytes, **deduplicates** by content hash, and folds the result into the *same* **`combine_attestations`** under the same freshness, revocation, and **`[floor, 1]`** discipline — so gossip changes only *where the evidence comes from*, never *how it is weighed*: a directory-denied peer is **skipped and pinpointed**, the fan-out is bounded by `max_peers`, a forged artifact a peer serves is **refused**, a revocation a peer gossips **excludes** the withdrawn claim, and every peer visited and artifact fetched lands on the **audit chain** — never a hosted reputation registry or a push-based gossip bus |
| `76_cross_org_transitive_trust.py` | cross-org transitive trust & **Sybil-resistant** attestation weighting — weighing each issuer's evidence by the importer's **own trust in that issuer**, a bounded, transitive web-of-trust rooted in its local ledger (**`TrustConfig`** / **`build_trust_model`** / **`TrustModel`**, opt-in via **`combine_attestations(..., trust_config=)`** / `app.import_reputation` / `app.gather_reputation`): an issuer it knows first-hand pulls at full weight, an issuer a trusted one vouches for inherits trust **at most a hop away with decay**, and an unknown one is **floored, never zeroed** — so corroboration from a trusted peer counts for more than volume from a stranger, a chain beyond the depth bound manufactures no standing, and a cluster of mutually-vouching unknown **Sybil** issuers cannot outvote a few trusted ones (pull follows earned trust, not issuer count); the multiplier is bounded **`[floor, 1]`**, pinpointed (`SubjectStanding.issuer_trust` / `AttestationVerdict.trust`), reversible, and the prior still drops into the negotiation path unchanged — never a central trust authority |
| `77_cross_org_reputation_gated_admission.py` | cross-org reputation-gated admission & **progressive exposure** — making the weighted standing **act**, not merely be consulted: an **`AdmissionPolicy`** (**`app.admit`** / **`admit`**) maps a counterparty's **`PortableReputation`** / **`ReputationLedger`** standing to a bounded, offline-verifiable **`AdmissionDecision`** — a **maximum contract value** (the exposure ceiling), a required **escrow/collateral fraction**, and an **SLA-strictness factor**. Exposure is the product of *how good* the standing is (its posterior-mean reputation) and *how much corroborated, settled history* stands behind it, lifted off a floor — so a thin or low-trust standing is admitted on **conservative terms rather than refused** (discounted exposure, never a hard gate), the ceiling **ramps toward parity** as settled deliveries accrue and a regression **walks it back** (local first-hand evidence wins). Every decision **re-derives the terms from the bound standing** (`AdmissionDecision.verify` catches a tampered ceiling even after re-sealing), lands on the **hash-chained audit log**, and folds into the existing path (`bound_position` clamps a buyer's position to the ceiling, `apply_to_terms` caps and stamps the terms) — never a hosted underwriting service |
| `78_cross_org_collateralized_escrow.py` | cross-org collateralized settlement & **escrow** — giving the admission-required collateral **teeth**: an **`Escrow`** (**`app.post_escrow`** / **`post_escrow`**) binds the collateral fraction to a *specific* **`Contract`** and counterparty into a signed, offline-verifiable artifact (the escrow analogue of a `SettlementRecord`), the held amount **re-deriving from the admission posture**. Settling the contract **releases** the whole stake on a fulfilled delivery and **forfeits** a bounded, pinpointed slice **proportional to the shortfall** on a breach (**never the whole stake, never punitive**, the remainder released) — driven by the *same* settlement record verdict the books already close on (**`app.settle(escrow=…)`** / **`settle_escrow`**), so the collateral closes the same loop the settlement does. Every post, release, and forfeiture **recomputes from the bytes** (`Escrow.verify` catches a tampered amount or forfeiture even after re-sealing) and lands on the **hash-chained audit log** — never a hosted escrow custodian or a payment rail |
| `79_cross_org_collateral_pooling.py` | cross-org collateral pooling & **cross-contract margin** — a counterparty running many concurrent contracts would post **separate** collateral per deal even though its breaches and clean deliveries net out, stranding capital contract-by-contract. A **`CollateralPool`** (**`app.post_collateral_pool`** / **`post_collateral_pool`**) pools it: a **single posted stake** (a margin account) backs many contracts at a deterministic, offline-verifiable **allocation proportional to each contract's admission-required collateral** (the collateral analogue of a `NettingSet`). Settling a contract **draws** a bounded, pinpointed forfeiture from the **shared stake** on a breach and **releases** the rest back to the **available balance** on a clean delivery (**`app.settle(pool=…)`** / **`draw_pool`**), so a clean delivery **frees capital** for the next deal and a breach is **covered from the pool** — driven by the *same* settlement verdict the books close on. A pool committed below the collateral its open contracts require surfaces a bounded, pinpointed **top-up** obligation (`pool.top_up`) rather than silently over-committing. Every post, draw, release, and top-up **recomputes from the bytes** (`CollateralPool.verify` re-derives the allocations and reconciles the balance, catching a tampered allocation even after re-sealing) and lands on the **hash-chained audit log** — never a hosted clearing house, a margin custodian, or a payment rail |
| `80_cross_org_collateral_rehypothecation.py` | cross-org collateral **rehypothecation guards & re-use bounds** — a pool only re-allocates capital *within itself*, so nothing bounds a counterparty that pledges the **same** stake across more than one pool, double-counting what actually backs each deal. A **`CollateralLedger`** (**`app.guard_collateral`** / **`guard_collateral`**) is the **rehypothecation guard**: it folds a counterparty's pools into one view and reconciles what they collectively pledge against the capital it actually **holds**, surfacing the same capital pledged twice as a bounded, pinpointed **`ReuseBreach`** (a re-pledged contract) rather than silently over-stating coverage. When a stake backs deals for more than one beneficiary, each **`BeneficiaryClaim`** is bounded to its deterministic **pari-passu share** of the held capital, so a forfeiture cannot pay one beneficiary out of capital another has first claim on. The ledger reads only the signed, content-bound pools (a tampered one is **refused** at fold time), re-derives the re-use bound and the apportionment **from the bytes** (caught even after re-sealing), and lands the guard on the **hash-chained audit log** — never a hosted custodian, a clearing house, or a payment rail |
| `81_cross_org_custody_proof_of_reserves.py` | cross-org collateral **custody attestation & proof-of-reserves** — the rehypothecation guard bounds pledges against the capital a counterparty **holds**, but that figure was *asserted*, not proven, so a counterparty over-stating its reserves still passed. A tampered reserve figure, a forged custodian (with a verifier), or an attestation for a **different poster** is **refused**, the breach re-derives **from the bytes**, and every issuance and guard lands on the **hash-chained audit log** — never a hosted custodian or a proof-of-reserves auditor |
